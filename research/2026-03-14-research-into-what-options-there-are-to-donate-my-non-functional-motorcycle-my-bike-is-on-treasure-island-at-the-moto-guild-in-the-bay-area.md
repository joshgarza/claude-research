# Research into what options there are to donate my non-functional motorcycle. My bike is on Treasure Island at the Moto Guild in the bay area.

## Findings

### Bottom line
You have several viable donation paths, and the easiest path is usually **a Bay Area nonprofit that uses an outsourced vehicle-donation processor**. For a non-running motorcycle on Treasure Island, the practical constraints are not "Can I find any charity?" but rather:

1. **Is the title clear and available?**
2. **Is the bike complete, towable, and accessible to a tow truck?**
3. **Do I care most about supporting a specific local cause, or maximizing simplicity and pickup speed?**
4. **Do I care about tax deductibility enough to follow the IRS documentation rules carefully?**

For your exact situation, the strongest default recommendation is:

- Start with a **San Francisco-anchored or SF-friendly program that explicitly handles motorcycles and non-running vehicles**, especially **Habitat for Humanity Greater San Francisco Bay Area**, **Immigration Institute of the Bay Area**, **KALW**, or **Habitat for Humanity East Bay/Silicon Valley** ([Habitat GSF](https://habitatgsf.org/car-donation/); [IIBA](https://iibayarea.org/get-involved/car-donation/); [KALW](https://www.kalw.org/donate-your-vehicle); [Habitat EBSV](https://www.habitatebsv.org/support-us/ways-to-donate/donate-a-car-bay-area)).
- If mission fit matters more than the closest geographic match, **BACS**, **Save The Bay**, and **Hopalong** are also viable, but their published pages are slightly less direct about your exact Treasure Island pickup scenario ([BACS](https://bayareacs.org/donate-vehicle/); [Save The Bay](https://savesfbay.org/vehicle-donations/); [Hopalong](https://hopalong.careasy.org/home)).
- Before scheduling, confirm with **Moto Guild** that the motorcycle can be released during the pickup window. Moto Guild lists its Treasure Island address as **849 13th Street, Treasure Island, San Francisco, CA 94130** and publishes a phone number and email, so arranging a tow pickup there is logistically realistic ([Moto Guild](https://www.motoguild-sf.com/)).
- Tell the donation processor up front that the bike is **non-running** and disclose whether it has inflated tires, an engine, and clear tow access.

### The hard constraints that determine whether your bike is donatable

#### 1. Title status is the first gate
California DMV says a valid California title proves ownership, and any change in ownership or lienholder must be reported to DMV. The DMV title-transfer guidance explicitly includes **donation** as a transfer type, and sellers must also submit a **Notice of Transfer and Release of Liability (NRL)** within **5 days** ([California DMV, Title Transfers and Changes](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/vehicle-registration/titles/title-transfers-and-changes/); [California DMV, NRL manual page](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/handbook/vehicle-industry-registration-procedures-manual-2/transfers/notice-of-transfer-and-release-of-liability-nrl-irl-reg-138/)).

The dominant donation processor in this space, **CARS/Careasy**, says you generally need a **current and clear title**, and any lienholder on the title must be cleared or released ([Careasy Vehicle Donor FAQ](https://careasy.org/vehicle-donor/faqs)).

Practical implication: if you do not have the title, or if there is a lien still showing, solve that first. Otherwise the donation will likely stall.

#### 2. "Non-running" is usually fine, but "incomplete or inaccessible" may not be
This is the most important practical point for your bike.

Careasy's donor FAQ says it can accept most vehicles **running or not**, but the vehicle must generally:

- be **in one piece**
- **have an engine**
- be **towable**
- be **tow truck accessible**

It also says tow operators typically need a direct path to the vehicle and may not be able to handle blocked, underground, or otherwise awkward pickup situations ([Careasy Vehicle Donor FAQ](https://careasy.org/vehicle-donor/faqs)).

Habitat East Bay/Silicon Valley's donation pages reinforce the same pattern. Habitat says it accepts **motorcycles, whether they are running or not**, with free towing, but its ReStore guidance says **incomplete vehicles** and vehicles **without four inflated tires for towing** are not accepted ([Habitat EBSV Cars for Homes](https://www.habitatebsv.org/support-us/ways-to-donate/donate-a-car-bay-area); [Habitat EBSV ReStore donation list](https://restore.habitatebsv.org/donate/)).

Practical implication for Treasure Island: a non-functional motorcycle is still a good candidate **if it is complete and can be physically loaded**. If it is partly disassembled, missing the engine, or buried in a way that a truck cannot reach, some programs may reject it.

#### 3. Smog should not be the blocker here
California DMV says motorcycles do **not** require a smog check before registration, and both KALW and Careasy say donated vehicles generally do not need smog or safety inspections before pickup. For this bike, title/access/towability are much more likely blockers than emissions paperwork ([California DMV, Register a Motorcycle](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/vehicle-registration/new-registration/register-a-motorcycle/); [KALW Donate Your Vehicle](https://www.kalw.org/donate-your-vehicle); [Careasy Vehicle Donor FAQ](https://careasy.org/vehicle-donor/faqs)).

### Current operating pattern in 2026: charity front-end, shared vehicle-donation back-end
The clearest pattern across the Bay Area programs I reviewed is that most charities are **not** directly dispatching their own tow trucks or reselling vehicles themselves. Instead, the charity-branded page routes into a shared processor: IIBA uses **CARS**, BACS routes donors into **Careasy/CARS**, KALW says **CARS** operates its program, Hopalong uses **Careasy**, Habitat GSF routes through Habitat's **Cars for Homes** program, and Save The Bay routes into a vehicle-donation service ([IIBA](https://iibayarea.org/get-involved/car-donation/); [BACS](https://bayareacs.org/donate-vehicle/); [KALW](https://www.kalw.org/donate-your-vehicle); [Hopalong](https://hopalong.careasy.org/home); [Habitat GSF](https://habitatgsf.org/car-donation/); [Save The Bay](https://savesfbay.org/vehicle-donations/)).

Observed consequence: the real decision is usually less "which Bay Area charity will take a motorcycle?" and more:

1. Which cause do you want to support?
2. Which shared processor will accept your bike's title, condition, and access constraints?

That is why the acceptance criteria converge around clear title, towability, accessibility, and eventual sale/salvage rather than direct reuse by the nonprofit.

### Concrete Bay Area-compatible donation options

#### Option 1: Habitat for Humanity Greater San Francisco Bay Area
Because Treasure Island is in San Francisco, this is probably the cleanest first call. Habitat Greater San Francisco says vehicle donations support **San Francisco, San Mateo, and Marin counties**, that it accepts **motorcycles whether they are running or not**, and that **towing is free**. It also says all submissions are subject to evaluation due to **donation location and vehicle condition**, and explains the likely disposition up front: about half of accepted vehicles go through wholesale auctions and about half go to salvage yards ([Habitat GSF car donation](https://habitatgsf.org/car-donation/)).

Why this is strong:

- best geographic fit for a Treasure Island pickup
- explicit acceptance of motorcycles
- explicit acceptance of non-running vehicles
- explicit free towing
- clearer than most pages about what happens after pickup

Watch-out:

- Habitat GSF says every submission is evaluated for location and condition, so Treasure Island access still needs to work for the tow vendor ([Habitat GSF car donation](https://habitatgsf.org/car-donation/)).

Recommendation: if the bike is whole, title-clear, and can roll for pickup, this is the best first inquiry to send. If Habitat GSF declines due location/condition, **Habitat EBSV** is the strongest backup because its local page also explicitly says motorcycles are accepted whether running or not with free towing, while its ReStore guidance warns that incomplete or untowable vehicles may be refused ([Habitat EBSV Cars for Homes](https://www.habitatebsv.org/support-us/ways-to-donate/donate-a-car-bay-area); [Habitat EBSV ReStore donation list](https://restore.habitatebsv.org/donate/)).

#### Option 2: Immigration Institute of the Bay Area (IIBA)
IIBA is San Francisco-based and says **all vehicles are accepted regardless of condition**. Its page explains that its partner **CARS (Charitable Adult Rides & Services)** coordinates a **free pickup**, sells the vehicle, and sends the donor a thank-you letter serving as a tax receipt ([IIBA car donation page](https://iibayarea.org/get-involved/car-donation/); [IIBA ways to donate page](https://iibayarea.org/get-involved/ways-to-donate/)).

Why this is strong:

- explicitly local to San Francisco
- explicit "regardless of condition" language
- outsourced pickup and sale handled for you

Trade-off:

- because the sale is outsourced through CARS, this is almost certainly an **auction/sale-proceeds** model rather than a direct reuse-by-the-charity model.

Recommendation: if you want a San Francisco nonprofit and minimal hassle, this is a very good option.

#### Option 3: Bay Area Community Services (BACS)
BACS has one of the most transparent pages I found. It says:

- enter **BACS** as the charity of choice at Careasy
- CARS will check ownership papers and load the vehicle
- the vehicle can be in **any condition**
- **70% of the proceeds** from the sale of the vehicle go to BACS

Source: [BACS Donate a Vehicle](https://bayareacs.org/donate-vehicle/).

Why this is strong:

- Bay Area mission alignment
- explicit "any condition" language
- unusually good transparency about proceeds

Trade-off:

- BACS explicitly discloses that this is a split-proceeds program. If maximizing the exact share that reaches the charity matters, BACS is at least transparent, but you should not assume every dollar of sale value reaches BACS.

Recommendation: this is a strong choice if you want Bay Area social-services impact and you care about dealing with a program that publicly explains where the proceeds go.

#### Option 4: Save The Bay
Save The Bay still maintains a current vehicle-donations page, and an older but still-live program post says the vehicle **doesn't need to be in running order**, that the pickup company will **pick up the vehicle directly**, and that the program accepts **motorcycles**. Proceeds support Bay restoration and climate-related work in the region ([Save The Bay vehicle donations](https://savesfbay.org/vehicle-donations/); [Save The Bay, Wheels for Wetlands](https://savesfbay.org/donate-your-wheels-for-wetlands/)).

Why this is strong:

- Bay Area environmental mission
- explicit non-running acceptance
- explicit motorcycle acceptance
- pickup handled for you

Trade-off:

- the published page is older than some of the other sources, so I would confirm the current partner and process when you call.

Recommendation: very good if your main goal is to support a Bay-focused environmental cause.

#### Option 5: Hopalong Animal Rescue
Hopalong says it serves shelters throughout the **Bay Area** and accepts **motorcycles** with **free towing** through its vehicle donation page ([Hopalong Animal Rescue vehicle donation page](https://hopalong.careasy.org/home)).

Why this is strong:

- Bay Area animal-rescue mission
- motorcycle explicitly listed
- free towing

Trade-off:

- the public landing page does not spell out the exact non-running/towability criteria itself. Because it uses a Careasy flow, it is reasonable to infer that the underlying towability rules are similar to Careasy's general donor FAQ, but that is still an inference rather than a sentence Hopalong itself prints on the page.

Recommendation: good option if the bike is complete and towable and you want the donation to support animal rescue.

#### Option 6: KALW vehicle donation
KALW is stronger than many public-radio donation pages because it publishes the actual acceptance rules. Its page says it accepts **most vehicles, running or not, including motorcycles**, offers **no-cost pickup**, and expects donated vehicles to be **in one piece**, **towable**, **have an engine**, and be **accessible by a tow truck**. It also tells donors to have the **title** and **VIN** ready and says **CARS** operates the program; after pickup, sale processing may take roughly **4 to 12 weeks** before the final acknowledgment arrives ([KALW Donate Your Vehicle](https://www.kalw.org/donate-your-vehicle)).

Why this is strong:

- very local mission, supporting San Francisco public radio
- explicit motorcycle + non-running acceptance
- clear towability/access rules
- clear practical instructions and timeline

Trade-off:

- it is still a CARS resale pipeline, so the tax result usually follows what the bike eventually sells for, not what you hope it is worth.

Recommendation: this is a real first-tier option if you want the beneficiary to be very local to San Francisco and the bike meets the published towability/access rules.

#### Option 7: A national fallback if your preferred Bay Area nonprofit is not already listed
Careasy says you **can donate to a nonprofit not listed** on its site by submitting the name and address of your preferred nonprofit, and it will contact them for approval. It also says pickup can often be scheduled quickly, often within **2 to 3 business days** ([Careasy FAQ](https://careasy.org/faq1); [Careasy Vehicle Donor FAQ](https://careasy.org/vehicle-donor/faqs)).

This matters because it means the real option set is broader than just the public Bay Area donation pages you happen to find in search. If there is a nonprofit you care about more than the ones above, it may still be possible to donate the bike through the same backend infrastructure.

### One concrete non-fit: KPFA appears worse for a non-functional motorcycle
KPFA's donation page is useful as a warning example. It says a car does **not always** have to be running, but its accepted-vehicles list specifically says it takes **motorcycles (if they are operational)** ([KPFA vehicle donation page](https://kpfa.org/donate-your-vehicle/)).

Practical implication: if your bike is truly non-functional, KPFA is not the best first call.

### Tax and documentation reality, do not overestimate the write-off

#### The IRS rule is usually sale price, not your guess of "what the bike is worth"
IRS guidance is straightforward here. For a **qualified vehicle donation** with a claimed value over **$500**, you generally cannot claim the deduction unless you attach the contemporaneous written acknowledgment from the donee organization. The IRS says that acknowledgment is timely if furnished within **30 days** of the sale or, in certain exception cases, within **30 days** of the contribution ([IRS FAQ on charitable vehicle contributions](https://www.irs.gov/faqs/itemized-deductions-standard-deduction/gifts-charitable-contributions/gifts-charitable-contributions); [IRS Instructions for Form 8283](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i8283); [IRS Publication 526](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p526)).

The important nuance is that the usual rule for vehicle-donation programs that **sell** the vehicle is: **your deduction tracks the gross sale proceeds**, not your own estimate of fair market value. The IRS instructions describe exceptions when the charity materially improves the vehicle, makes significant intervening use of it, or gives/sells it at a price significantly below fair market value to a needy individual in furtherance of charity ([IRS Instructions for Form 8283](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i8283)).

Because nearly all of the programs above describe a resale/auction pipeline, the practical expectation should be:

- the bike is picked up
- it is sold or scrapped
- you receive the final acknowledgment / Form 1098-C-equivalent documentation
- your deduction usually follows that amount if you itemize

That is not just a generic assumption. Habitat GSF says about half of its accepted vehicles go through wholesale auctions and about half go to salvage yards, and KALW says vehicles are sold through used-car markets and auction facilities before the donor receives the final paperwork ([Habitat GSF car donation](https://habitatgsf.org/car-donation/); [KALW Donate Your Vehicle](https://www.kalw.org/donate-your-vehicle)).

This is important because for a non-running motorcycle, the tax outcome may be modest. The reason to donate should mostly be **convenience + support for a cause**, not the hope of a giant write-off.

### California-specific steps you should expect after pickup

#### 1. Submit the Notice of Transfer and Release of Liability
California DMV says the NRL protects you by telling DMV you no longer own the vehicle, and the seller/transferor is required to submit it within **five calendar days** of sale, gift, donation, or other disposal ([California DMV NRL FAQ](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/vehicle-registration/titles/notice-of-transfer-and-release-of-liability-nrl-faqs/); [California DMV Important Vehicle Information](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/important-vehicle-information/)).

This matters even if the charity or processor handles the title transfer later. The DMV is explicit that submitting an NRL **does not itself transfer ownership**, but it does protect you from later parking tickets, tolls, or other liability while the transfer works through the system ([California DMV NRL page](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/vehicle-registration/titles/title-transfers-and-changes/notice-of-transfer-and-release-of-liability-nrl/)).

#### 2. Keep every receipt and acknowledgment
Keep:

- the pickup receipt
- any temporary receipt from the tow driver
- the final sale acknowledgment / tax receipt
- any DMV paperwork the driver leaves

If the vehicle sells for more than $500 and you want the deduction, the IRS documentation is not optional ([IRS FAQ](https://www.irs.gov/faqs/itemized-deductions-standard-deduction/gifts-charitable-contributions/gifts-charitable-contributions); [IRS Publication 526](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p526)).

### A practical decision framework for your exact case

#### Best choice if you want the easiest yes for a non-running bike
Start with **Habitat GSF**, **IIBA**, and **KALW**. If Habitat GSF or KALW reject because of access or condition, try **Habitat EBSV** next.

Reasoning:

- all four are Bay Area-relevant
- all four have explicit, current pages
- Habitat GSF is the best geographic fit for Treasure Island
- IIBA explicitly says all vehicles are accepted regardless of condition
- KALW explicitly says motorcycles are accepted whether running or not if they meet the towability rules
- Habitat EBSV is the best explicit backup if the SF-county option declines

#### Best choice if you care most about a Bay Area mission match
Choose by cause:

- **housing / families**: Habitat GSF or Habitat EBSV
- **immigrant services**: IIBA
- **homelessness / behavioral health**: BACS
- **Bay environment**: Save The Bay
- **animals**: Hopalong
- **local public media**: KALW

#### Best choice if you care most about proceeds transparency
Call **BACS** first, because its page explicitly says **70% of proceeds** go to BACS. Many other programs do not publish their split publicly, so if that matters, ask each charity or processor directly.

#### Best choice if the bike is borderline towable
Use the CARS/Careasy rules as your screening checklist before you call:

- in one piece
- engine present
- towable
- accessible to a truck

If the answer is "not really," then the honest fallback is probably **sell/junk it and donate the cash**. That fallback is an inference from the acceptance criteria above, not a rule from a single official page, but it is the cleanest path when the vehicle itself no longer fits donation-program logistics.

### Recommended action order
1. Call or email **Moto Guild** to confirm when a tow operator can access the bike at **849 13th Street** and whether you or staff need to be present ([Moto Guild](https://www.motoguild-sf.com/)).
2. Check whether you have a **clear title**. If not, solve that before anything else ([California DMV Title Transfers](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/vehicle-registration/titles/title-transfers-and-changes/); [Careasy Vehicle Donor FAQ](https://careasy.org/vehicle-donor/faqs)).
3. Submit inquiries to **Habitat GSF**, **IIBA**, and **KALW** first. Those are the best-documented fits for a Treasure Island motorcycle donation.
4. If Habitat GSF or KALW reject for access or condition, try **Habitat EBSV** and **BACS** next. If environmental mission matters more than location fit, move **Save The Bay** up your list.
5. When you schedule, explicitly say: "Non-running motorcycle, complete, located at Moto Guild on Treasure Island, need tow pickup."
6. After pickup, file the **California NRL within 5 days** and keep the confirmation ([California DMV NRL FAQ](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/vehicle-registration/titles/notice-of-transfer-and-release-of-liability-nrl-faqs/)).
