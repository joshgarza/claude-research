# Almac Group and Director of Business Development interview prep

## Context
This note was prepared to support interview prep for an Almac Group Director of Business Development opening in the Pharma Services division covering the West Coast territory. The goal was to understand the company, the role, and the skills or positioning that should be emphasized in interviews.

## Findings

### Executive summary
Almac is a large, established, privately owned pharmaceutical services company with a broad drug-development footprint and a strong client-service narrative. The Director of Business Development role is best understood as a senior, quota-carrying field sales role for Almac Pharma Services, not primarily a people-management role. The strongest interview positioning is to present as a technically credible consultative seller who can build territory pipeline, qualify complex opportunities, shape proposals with internal teams, and grow long-term client relationships in a regulated pharma environment.

### Company overview
- Almac describes itself as a global leader across the drug development lifecycle, spanning R&D, diagnostic services, API manufacture, formulation development, clinical trial supply, commercial-scale manufacture, and distribution. ([About Us](https://www.almacgroup.com/about/?cc=US))
- As of the public company pages reviewed on April 23, 2026, Almac reports more than 7,800 employees, 18 facilities, more than 55 years of experience, and relationships with the top 25 biopharma companies. ([Homepage](https://www.almacgroup.com/), [About Us](https://www.almacgroup.com/about/?cc=US))
- Almac says it contributed to approximately 50% of FDA-approved new molecular entities in the last five years on its About page, and approximately 50% of approved NMEs in the last six years on its homepage. The exact denominator shifts by page and date, but the commercial point is consistent: Almac wants to be seen as a major, trusted partner to large biopharma. ([About Us](https://www.almacgroup.com/about/?cc=US), [Homepage](https://www.almacgroup.com/))
- Almac is privately owned and repeatedly emphasizes that it reinvests profits back into the business. ([FY2024 results announced May 28, 2025](https://www.almacgroup.com/news/almac-group-surpasses-1-billion-revenue-with-latest-annual-accounts-reporting-record-revenues-profit-and-employee-numbers/))

### Company values and culture signals
- Almac's published core values are Outstanding Quality, Exceptional Innovation, Superlative Customer Focus, Inspirational People, and Financial Performance. ([Core Values & Vision](https://www.almacgroup.com/about/core-values-vision/))
- The customer-facing language consistently emphasizes partnership, long-term relationships, responsiveness, quality, and tailored solutions. ([Homepage](https://www.almacgroup.com/), [Core Values & Vision](https://www.almacgroup.com/about/core-values-vision/))

### The specific Director of Business Development role

  - managing existing client relationships where appropriate

  - supporting the Proposals group in analyzing inquiries and generating proposals
  - understanding broader group capabilities well enough to cross-sell
  - developing and optimizing territory business plans in Salesforce
  - coordinating with internal teams such as project management, operations, design, logistics, and procurement to build proposals and align execution expectations
  - working with internal pharma departments globally to ensure client requirements are clearly understood before work begins
  ([Current role posting](https://www.almacgroup.com/careers/job/8a78879e9d4aea52019d552a9c981362/))
- Required background includes a life science or business degree, prior business development, account management, or technical project management experience in pharma, and a strong grasp of sales strategy, forecasting, funnel management, and account management. ([Current role posting](https://www.almacgroup.com/careers/job/8a78879e9d4aea52019d552a9c981362/))

### My read on the role
- The title says "Director", but the responsibilities read like an individual-contributor field sales role with meaningful internal coordination responsibility. This is an inference from the posting, not a statement Almac makes directly. ([Current role posting](https://www.almacgroup.com/careers/job/8a78879e9d4aea52019d552a9c981362/))
- The interviewer is likely to care less about abstract business-development language and more about whether the candidate can reliably move technical service opportunities from first meeting through proposal, internal alignment, and close.
- This looks like a role for someone who can sell into biotech and pharma decision-makers while staying grounded in operational reality. A candidate who oversells or lacks technical fluency would be risky in this environment.

### What should be played up in interviews

#### 1. Technical-commercial fluency
He should present himself as someone who can talk credibly about drug product development, manufacturing, testing, packaging, clinical supply, or adjacent outsourced pharma services without pretending to be the scientist in the room. The ideal posture is "I know enough to qualify well, frame the solution correctly, and bring the right internal experts in early."

#### 2. Consultative sales, not generic selling
The posting is explicit that the job includes market research, pipeline building, account planning, proposal support, and cross-functional coordination. That points to consultative selling through long, technical sales cycles. He should emphasize examples where he diagnosed a client problem, aligned internal stakeholders, and closed a solution that depended on multiple technical or operational teams.

#### 3. Territory planning and pipeline discipline
This role owns a territory and uses Salesforce for planning and analysis. He should highlight:
- account segmentation and prioritization
- target identification in biotech and pharma accounts
- pipeline hygiene and stage discipline
- forecast accuracy
- concrete examples of building a book of business in a defined region

#### 4. Ability to manage complex proposal cycles
The posting repeatedly mentions proposal support and liaison with operations, project management, logistics, procurement, and design. That is a strong clue that Almac needs someone who can convert vague customer requests into executable proposals without creating delivery problems later.

#### 5. Client trust and relationship depth
Almac's public language leans heavily on partnership, customer focus, and long-term relationships. He should have stories that show:
- revenue growth within existing accounts
- recovery of at-risk accounts
- clear expectation-setting with clients
- balancing responsiveness with operational feasibility

#### 6. Quality and regulatory awareness
Even though this is a business-development role, pharma services sales lives inside a quality and compliance context. He should signal respect for GMP environments, change control, realistic timelines, and why accuracy matters in scoping and commitments.

#### 7. West Coast market familiarity
Because this is a West Coast territory role, any credible relationships or familiarity with West Coast biotech hubs, especially the Bay Area and San Diego, should be highlighted. This is an inference from the territory definition and common pharma geography, but it is a practical selling point for the role. ([Current role posting](https://www.almacgroup.com/careers/job/8a78879e9d4aea52019d552a9c981362/))

### Strong positioning statements
Useful themes for interview answers:
- "I build technical pipeline, not just activity. I qualify hard, bring in the right experts early, and make sure what we propose is something operations can actually deliver."
- "I am comfortable selling complex outsourced pharma services because I can translate between client goals and internal execution realities."
- "I treat forecast discipline, account planning, and proposal quality as part of the sales job, not admin work after the fact."
- "I focus on long-term account value and trust, because in pharma services the next deal depends on how well the first one was scoped and delivered."

### Likely interview angles
Questions he should be ready for:
- How would you build a 30-60-90 day plan for a West Coast territory?
- How do you qualify whether a program is a good fit for Almac Pharma Services?
- Tell me about a complex sale that required multiple internal stakeholders to align before the client could be quoted.
- How do you balance hunting for new business with growing existing accounts?
- How do you avoid overpromising when the client wants aggressive timelines?
- How do you approach cross-selling broader services once an initial relationship is established?

### Good questions for him to ask
- What mix of this territory is expected to come from net-new logos versus expansion of existing accounts?
- Which Pharma Services capabilities are the biggest growth priority on the West Coast right now?
- How does Almac define success in the first six months for this role?
- How are proposal development and technical subject-matter experts involved during early-stage qualification?
- Where do new hires usually struggle, technical depth, territory focus, internal coordination, or deal qualification?

## Open questions
- Who is the direct manager for this role, and how centralized is BD leadership under the VP of Business Development announced in June 2024? ([VP of Business Development appointment](https://www.almacgroup.com/news/almac-pharma-services-appoints-new-vp-business-development/))
- What is the expected split between hunting and farming in the West Coast territory?
- Which specific service lines are the top growth focus: development, commercial manufacturing, packaging, analytical, or broader integrated solutions?
- How much travel is expected, and how much of the role is remote versus in-person field engagement?
- How aggressively does Almac expect this role to cross-sell other group capabilities beyond core Pharma Services?
